# NaviFreight: Logistics Intelligence  
### Optimizing the Ethiopia–Djibouti Coffee Export Corridor  

---

## Project Description  
NaviFreight is a specialized, data-driven logistics engine designed to automate and protect the export operations.  

The system synchronizes booking data from the “Big Three” ocean carriers — CMA CGM, MSC, and Maersk — and identifies critical weight discrepancies between the Mojo Dry Port (Inland) and the Djibouti Port (Loading).

---

## The Problem: The "VGM Gap"  

In the Ethiopian coffee export sector, manual data entry across multiple carrier platforms (Excel, CSV, API) creates significant inconsistencies in Verified Gross Mass (VGM) documentation.

- Error Rate: 17%  
- Operational Issue: Containers get *“rolled”* (left behind at port)  
- Business Impact: 
  - Estimated $2.1M annual loss  
  - Demurrage fees  
  - Contract penalties  
  - Coffee quality degradation due to heat exposure  

---

## The Solution: Tri-Carrier Normalization  

NaviFreight introduces a Python-based Normalization Engine that acts as a single source of truth:

### Core Features  
- Automated Data Harvesting  
  - CMA CGM - XLSX  
  - MSC - CSV  
  - Maersk - JSON  

- **VGM Shield**  
  - Flags containers where weight variance exceeds 50kg maritime tolerance 

- **Executive Cockpit**  
  - Interactive Streamlit dashboard 
  - Real-time monitoring of:  
    - Revenue at Risk  
    - Carrier reliability

## Recommendations to Romina PLC Management  

Based on insights generated by NaviFreight:

### 1. Digital Gate-Check  
Implement verification at the Mojo Dry Port stage instead of waiting for Djibouti port arrival.

### 2. Carrier Accountability  
Leverage the Carrier Reliability Index to negotiate better service agreements with high-performing carriers (Maersk, CMA CGM).

### 3. Cost Recovery Strategy  
Eliminating the 31 detected errors can recover $155,000, which can be reinvested into:  
- Warehouse expansion  
- Coffee processing upgrades
